# Protocol: Launch Architecture & GTM Physics
> **Focus**: Orchestrating momentum, distribution, and product adoption.
> **Resonance Phase**: Launch / Distribution

## 1. The Strategy of Repeated Impact
The best companies do not launch once. A single launch is a spike of entropy. You must design a system to capture attention across multiple phases and compound it over time. 
*   **The Goal**: Turn temporary attention into an *Owned Relationship* (Email, Account, Community).
*   **The Rule**: Never rely entirely on rented algorithms (Twitter, Product Hunt). Use them only as funnels back into your owned ecosystem.

## 2. The O.R.B. Distribution Model
Map your distribution strategy across these three vectors prior to launch:

### A. Owned Channels (The Core)
*   *Definition*: Direct access to your audience without algorithmic interference.
*   *Examples*: Email list, Blog, Discord/Slack community, In-app notifications.
*   *Tactic*: This is where the highest LTV users live. Drive all external traffic here.

### B. Rented Channels (The Funnel)
*   *Definition*: High-scale platforms where you trade data for reach.
*   *Examples*: Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Reddit, YouTube.
*   *Tactic*: Map content to the platform natively. Do not post links; post value natively and transition to your owned funnel at the end. Rented channels give speed, not stability.

### C. Borrowed Channels (The Accelerant)
*   *Definition*: Leveraging someone else's owned audience.
*   *Examples*: Co-marketing webinars, sponsorships, Newsletter crossovers, Influencer partnerships.
*   *Tactic*: Offer asymmetrical value. Target adjacent non-competitors who share your exact customer profile.

## 3. The 5-Phase Deployment Cycle
Launch is not an event; it is a sequence. Never deploy from 0 to 100. Follow the gradient:

### Phase 1: Internal Launch (The Crucible)
*   **Goal**: Find the fatal flaws.
*   **Action**: Unpolished prototype. Give to 5-10 friendly, highly-technical users. 

### Phase 2: Alpha (The Closed Loop)
*   **Goal**: Validate core architecture under minor stress.
*   **Action**: Invite-only. White-glove onboarding. The goal is qualitative feedback, not scale. 

### Phase 3: Beta (The Waitlist)
*   **Goal**: Build the scarcity engine and refine messaging.
*   **Action**: Launch the landing page without the product. Collect emails. Use the *Mimetic Desire* cognitive bias—people want what is exclusive.

### Phase 4: Early Access (The Gradient)
*   **Goal**: Controlled scale and data capture.
*   **Action**: Let users in batches (e.g., 100/day). Start charging immediately. Free users give different feedback than paying users. Leak "feature drops" on Rented channels to build momentum.

### Phase 5: General Availability (The Spike)
*   **Goal**: Maximum visibility and conversion.
*   **Action**: Open self-serve. Deploy the full O.R.B. stack. Product Hunt, Hacker News, Email Blasts. Treat this as an all-day event—respond to every single comment within 5 minutes.

## 4. Post-Launch Momentum (The Long Tail)
A launch fails if day 2 traffic is zero.
1.  **Automated Onboarding**: The immediate sequence to carry them to the "Aha!" moment.
2.  **The Changelog Loop**: Even small bug fixes should be published. It signals a living, breathing product.
3.  **The Matrix of Updates**:
    *   *Major* (New Products): Full campaign.
    *   *Medium* (Integrations): Segmented email.
    *   *Minor* (Bug fixes): Changelog.

## 5. The "Fail-Fast" Checklist
*   [ ] Does the landing page pass the 5-Second Test?
*   [ ] Is the onboarding flow fully automated?
*   [ ] Is Analytics tracking enabled (Activation events logged)?
*   [ ] Have we identified our top 3 Borrowed channels for the launch?
