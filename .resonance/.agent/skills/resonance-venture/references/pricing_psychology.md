# Protocol: Pricing Psychology & Strategy
> **Objective**: Optimize value perception, tier structure, and monetization strategy through behavioral economics and quantitative research.
> **Resonance Phase**: Venture Validation / Pricing 

## 1. The Three Pricing Axes
Pricing is not just a number on a page. It is a systematic architecture composed of three elements:
1.  **Packaging**: What's included at each tier? (Features vs Limits vs Support).
2.  **Pricing Metric**: What do you charge for? (Per user, per usage, flat fee).
3.  **Price Point**: How much do you charge? (The actual dollar amounts).

## 2. Value Metrics (The Core Engine)
A good value metric aligns the price with the value the customer receives. As they get more value, they pay more. It must be easy to understand and hard to game.
*   *SaaS/Collaboration*: Per seat (Slack, Notion).
*   *Infrastructure*: Per usage (AWS, Stripe).
*   *Marketing*: Per record (Mailchimp).

> 🔴 **Rule**: If a customer uses more of the product but doesn't get more value, your metric is wrong.

## 3. Quantitative Research Methods
Do not guess the price. Calculate the Willingness to Pay (WTP).
*   **Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter**: Ask four questions to find the optimal zone:
    1. At what price is it too expensive?
    2. At what price is it too cheap (doubt quality)?
    3. At what price is it expensive but acceptable?
    4. At what price is it a bargain?
*   **MaxDiff Analysis**: Show sets of features and ask forcing functions (Most important vs Least important) to construct tier packaging intelligently.

## 4. Psychological Formatting (The UX of Price)
Once the math is done, apply these cognitive biases to the pricing page display:
*   **Anchoring**: Always present the highest-priced option (or Enterprise) first. The first number seen dictates the perception of all following numbers.
*   **Decoy Effect**: Add an asymmetrical 3rd option to force a logical choice.
*   **Charm Pricing vs Rounded Pricing**: 
    *   Ends in 9 ($49): Signals a "good deal" and value.
    *   Ends in 0 ($500): Signals premium quality and luxury.
*   **Rule of 100**: For discounts under $100, use percentages ("20% off"). For products over $100, use absolute dollars ("$50 off") to maximize the psychological impact.
*   **Center-Stage Effect**: Users gravitate to the middle option. Make it your "Recommended" plan.
*   **Mental Accounting (The Pain of Paying)**: "Less than $1 a day" feels significantly cheaper than "$30 a month," despite being the same math. Minimize the frequency of the "pain of paying" by pushing annual plans.
