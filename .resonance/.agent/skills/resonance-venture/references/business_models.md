# The High-Leverage Models

Choose the vehicle that fits your Skills, Capital, and Risk Tolerance.

## 1. The Machine (Productized Service)
*   **Concept**: Fixed Scope + Fixed Price + AI Execution.
*   **Leverage**: Systems & AI.
*   **Pros**: Fast cash flow, high demand.
*   **Cons**: Requires operational discipline.
*   **Ideal For**: Designers, Writers, Developers who hate "meetings".

## 2. The Platform (Micro-SaaS)
*   **Concept**: Solving one niche problem with code.
*   **Leverage**: Code (Infinite scale).
*   **Pros**: Recurring revenue, sleep money.
*   **Cons**: High skill barrier, slow churn/growth initially.
*   **Ideal For**: Engineers who notice inefficiencies.

## 3. The Authority (Expert Business)
*   **Concept**: Selling high-ticket outcomes (Consulting/Coaching).
*   **Leverage**: Reputation & Media.
*   **Pros**: Highest margins, elite network.
*   **Cons**: Ties to your personal time (initially).
*   **Ideal For**: Industry veterans with a track record.

## 4. The Arbitrageur (Drop-Servicing)
*   **Concept**: Selling usage/results, fulfilling via white-label/AI/Contractors.
*   **Leverage**: Capital & labor arbitrage.
*   **Pros**: Very scalable, no delivery bottleneck.
*   **Cons**: Thin margins if not optimized.
*   **Ideal For**: Sales/Marketing pros.

## 5. The Publisher (Media/Info)
*   **Concept**: Audience First -> Digital Products/Sponsorships.
*   **Leverage**: Media (Zero marginal cost of replication).
*   **Pros**: Infinite margin.
*   **Cons**: Audience building takes time (6-18 months).
*   **Ideal For**: Charismatic creators, Writers.

## 6. The Acquisition (Buy then Build)
*   **Concept**: Buy a boring cash-flowing business, optimize with Tech.
*   **Leverage**: Capital (Skipping the "0 to 1" phase).
*   **Pros**: Immediate cash flow.
*   **Cons**: High capital requirement, due diligence risk.
*   **Ideal For**: Operators with capital to deploy.
