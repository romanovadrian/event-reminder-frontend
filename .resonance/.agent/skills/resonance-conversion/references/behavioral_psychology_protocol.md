# Protocol: Behavioral Engineering (Psychology)
> **Focus**: Cognitive Biases, Action Orchestration, & Decision Architecture
> **Resonance Phase**: Conversion / Strategy

## 1. The Human Operating System
We do not "persuade"; we align with existing cognitive architecture. A user will not convert if friction outweighs motivation.
**Core Axiom**: `Behavior = Motivation x Ability x Prompt` (Fogg B=MAT).

## 2. Foundational Mental Models (The Strategy Layer)
*   **First Principles**: Break problems down to fundamental truths (5 Whys). Stop copying competitor landing pages. Ask *why* the page exists.
*   **Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)**: Users do not buy "software." They "hire" your product to achieve a specific outcome. Emphasize the end state, not the features.
*   **Occam's Razor**: The simplest explanation is usually correct. If conversions drop, check for a broken button or slow page speed before assuming a complex attribution failure.
*   **Inversion**: Ask "What would guarantee failure?" Then systematically avoid those elements (e.g., confusing copy, slow load times, hidden pricing).

## 3. The Cognitive Bias Library (The API)

### A. Decision Heuristics (Quick Thinking)
1.  **Social Proof & Bandwagon Effect**: "Others are doing it, so it's safe."
    *   *Implementation*: Use numbers aggressively ("Joined by 10,000 teams", recent purchaser popups).
2.  **Scarcity & The Urgency Heuristic**: "If I don't act, I lose."
    *   *Implementation*: 🔴 **Must be ethical**. Use definite limits ("3 seats left"). Never use fake countdown timers.
3.  **Authority Bias**: "The expert says it's good."
    *   *Implementation*: Feature certs ("SOC2"), thought leaders, or "As seen in".

### B. Purchase Psychology
1.  **Anchoring**: The first number dictates all subsequent value perception. (See Pricing Protocol).
2.  **Hyperbolic Discounting (Present Bias)**: People strongly prefer immediate rewards over future ones. "Start saving time today" outperforms "See ROI in 6 months".
3.  **Zero-Price Effect**: Free is psychologically irrational. The jump from $1 to $0 is exponentially larger than the jump from $2 to $1. Leverage freemium/free trials heavily.
4.  **Endowment Effect / Sunk Cost**: People value things more once they own them or invest in them.
    *   *Implementation*: Free trials build ownership. "Import your data" creates an immediate sunk cost integration, drastically reducing churn.

### C. The Friction Layer
1.  **IKEA Effect**: Users disproportionately value what they build.
    *   *Implementation*: Let them configure their dashboard or select their goals during onboarding.
2.  **Paradox of Choice**: Too many options paralyze action.
    *   *Implementation*: Limit options. Three pricing tiers beat seven. One primary CTA beats three.
3.  **Status-Quo Bias**: Change requires extreme effort.
    *   *Implementation*: Radically reduce switching friction. Offer "One-click CSV imports" from competitors.

### D. Memory & Emotion
1.  **Peak-End Rule**: Users judge an experience by the peak (best or worst moment) and the end, not the average.
    *   *Implementation*: Design a delightful success state (confetti on purchase) and a memorable onboarding conclusion.
2.  **Pratfall Effect**: Competence becomes more likable when it shows a small flaw. Perfection is distrusted.
    *   *Implementation*: Admit a weakness ("We're not the cheapest, but we're the fastest") to spike credibility.
3.  **Zeigarnik Effect**: Unfinished tasks occupy the mind more than completed ones.
    *   *Implementation*: Show progress bars ("You're 80% done"). Open loops pull the user forward to complete onboarding.

## 4. Engineering "The Nudge"
A Nudge is a default choice architecture that alters behavior without forbidding options.
*   **Defaults**: The Default Effect is the most powerful force. Checkboxes should be pre-selected (if ethical). The target pricing tier should be marked "Recommended".
*   **Visual Hierarchy**: The "Primary Action" must carry significantly more visual weight than the "Secondary Action" to eliminate cognitive load.

## 5. Emotional Debugging
If a user is not converting, debug the emotion using the **5 Whys**.
*   *Why?* "Too expensive."
*   *Why?* "Don't see the ROI."
*   *Why?* "Don't trust the calculator."
*   *Fix*: Improve the calculator's UX, not the price.

## 6. Ethical Bounds (The Safety Rail)
We use psychology to help users make the decision *they already want to make*.
*   **Dark Patterns**: Banned. (e.g., Hidden unsubscribe, forced continuity, fake scarcity).
*   **Transparency**: Radical honesty builds higher LTV than trickery.
